A4 — Page anatomy: headers, heroes & controls are cards too

Companion to A4-card-grid. The point here: a page's title block, hero, and control bar are not free-floating chrome — they are cards on the same grid, just shorter. This mockup introduces the half-row height unit (≈ 5.5 rem, half of an 11 rem data row) and shows two patterns: controls riding inline in the page-header (Example A), versus a dedicated controls strip when there are many (Example B). Same glass tokens, radius, gap, container cap, and dusk-sky backdrop as the sibling mockup.

New page-anatomy pieces

page-header card full-width (4-col), half-row tall — holds page title + short info. On the Now page it doubles as the hero.
controls strip full-width, half-row card holding dropdowns / tabs / buttons. Its own card when controls don't fit inline.
half-row ≈ 5.5rem tall — visibly half of a full data-row (11rem). Used for headers, heroes, and control strips.
full data-row 11rem — the normal content card height (1 row).
Rule: few controls → ride inline in the page-header (Example A). Many controls → get a dedicated controls strip (Example B).
No free-floating text or buttons — title, info, and controls are all cards.
Half-rows pack with zero waste — the grid track is the half-row (5.5rem); data cards span 2 tracks, tall cards 4, strips 1. Two stacked strips = one data-row, no gap.
